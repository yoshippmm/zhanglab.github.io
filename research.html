RESEARCH

Peng Zhang received his Ph.D. in Bioinformatics from the Institute of Biophysics, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and trained as a postdoctoral research associate in the Childrenâ€™s National Medical Center. Dr. Zhang is currently an Assitant Professor with the Division of Immunotherapy and the Director of Bioinformatics Core at the Institute of Human Virology, University of Maryland School of Medicine. He is a computational biologist with expertise in biomedical informatics, cancer genomics, and immunogenomics. His research focuses on integrative mining and framework development from sequencing data generated on high throughput techniques in tumor development, progression, drug response, and resistance.

(1) the development of novel computational frameworks to systematically analysis of tumor immunity to inform effective cancer immunotherapies;

(2) the utilization of single-cell sequencing technologies to uncover both systematic trends and specific elements influencing oncogenic processes, tumor microenvironment, and drug responses;

(3) the application of cutting-edge genomic and informatics technologies to solve important problems in the bio-medical field

Research/Clinical Keywords

Bioinformatics, Cancer Genomics, Immunogenomics, Single-Cell Sequencing
